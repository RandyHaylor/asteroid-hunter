A new and primary game mechanic, replacing the 'take cover behind an asteroid' one. here's a tag line for the intro screen:
"In space flight, turning is expensive. There's no 'air' to push against. Ships have advanced tractor beam technology to slingshot around asteroids as a means of rapidly changing direction."

Ships will simply always have forward momentum. Thrust doesn't change it, it just changes your direction vector to slowly move towards the ship direction. There is no 'fake air space steering like airplanes in atmosphere'

Ships freely rotate independent of their movement trajectory, including the player.  Ships turn and boost to change their direction slightly. 

Basic enemy ships can change direction well but not use asteroid grappling turns. harder enemies have better and stronger asteroid grappling abilities as the enemy type progresses. We'll have three enemy types, one that can steer ok but not grapple at all, one that can steer a little and grapple but not very intensely (still hard to auto-track though during the grappling), and advanced variants can be fast with strong grapple but weak turning, etc.

remove the cover system and the extra joystick used for moving around the asteroid. remove the throttle control and place a single thrust button that will cause a slow change in direction when pressed (it will be in the bottom left corner relative to radar).  Around the radar sphere, there will appear icons representing in-range asteroids you can slingshot around/orbit. the color will change from yellow through orange to red for how close the asteroid is/how rapid/strong the slingshot will be.   Asteroids that are too close cannot be used (shown black, untappable - asteroids too far don't show at all). Tapping an active asteroid latches onto it, causing the player ship to orbit the asteroid. tapping again releases. tapping and holding causes orbit, a held tap over 1s causes an end to the orbit on release. if orbiting, tapping another asteroid button switches instantly to orbiting that asteroid instead. 

Pin for later implemenation: Grapple based asteroid avoidance - if a ship is going to hit an asteroid, an automatic system pushes it in a deflection path - a fuzzy white line appears between ship and asteroid. this starts when on a collision path and getting somewhat close to the asteroid)
